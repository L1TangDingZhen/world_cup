# Full-pipeline tournament backtests: 2018 & 2022 World Cups

Date: 2026-07-13 · 10,000 simulations per run, seed 42.

The single-match backtests validate match probabilities; this experiment
validates everything the simulator stacks on top of them — group ranking
rules, bracket wiring and Monte Carlo aggregation. For each past World Cup
the model is trained **only on matches played before the opening day**, the
whole tournament is simulated with the era's own rules (32 teams, 8 groups,
pre-2026 tie-breakers: overall goal difference before head-to-head), and
the per-stage advancement probabilities are scored against what really
happened.

**Skill score** = 1 − Brier(model) / Brier(baseline), where the baseline
("climatology") gives every team the structural probability of reaching a
stage (16/32 for the round of 16, …, 1/32 for champion). Positive skill
means the pipeline knows more than the bracket structure itself.

## Results

### 2018 (train < 2018-06-14, 41,639 matches)

| Stage reached | Elo-Poisson skill | Dixon-Coles skill |
|---|---|---|
| Round of 16 | 0.337 | **0.461** |
| Quarter-final | 0.100 | **0.227** |
| Semi-final | −0.019 | **0.033** |
| Final | −0.091 | −0.075 |
| Champion | −0.044 | −0.053 |

Champion (France, pre-tournament 5th favourite): Elo-Poisson gave 6.1%,
Dixon-Coles 6.2% (baseline 3.1%); log score 2.81 / 2.79 vs baseline 3.47.

### 2022 (train < 2022-11-20, 45,700 matches)

| Stage reached | Elo-Poisson skill | Dixon-Coles skill |
|---|---|---|
| Round of 16 | 0.110 | 0.103 |
| Quarter-final | 0.335 | **0.367** |
| Semi-final | 0.076 | 0.006 |
| Final | **0.224** | 0.117 |
| Champion | **0.260** | 0.093 |

Champion (Argentina): Elo-Poisson gave 21.7%, Dixon-Coles 13.9%
(baseline 3.1%); log score 1.53 / 1.97 vs baseline 3.47.

### Averaged over both tournaments

| Stage reached | Elo-Poisson skill | Dixon-Coles skill |
|---|---|---|
| Round of 16 | 0.224 | **0.282** |
| Quarter-final | 0.218 | **0.297** |
| Semi-final | 0.029 | 0.020 |
| Final | 0.067 | 0.021 |
| Champion | 0.108 | 0.020 |
| Champion log score (baseline 3.47) | **2.17** | 2.38 |

## Conclusions

1. **The full pipeline has genuine skill.** Averaged over both
   tournaments, both models beat the structure-only baseline at every
   stage, and both priced the actual champion well above 1/32 in both
   editions. The simulator layer (rules, brackets, aggregation) does not
   destroy the information in the match model.
2. **Dixon-Coles is clearly better at the stages with the most signal**
   (round-of-16 qualification, quarter-final reach) — consistent with its
   better single-match calibration.
3. **Deep-round skills are dominated by single-tournament luck.** Each
   edition has only 1–4 positive outcomes per deep stage: chaotic 2018
   (Germany out in groups, Croatia in the final) produced slightly
   negative deep-round skills for both models, favourite-friendly 2022
   strongly positive ones. Elo's higher champion skill rests almost
   entirely on having priced Argentina 2022 very aggressively (21.7%);
   with n = 2 champions this is not evidence that Elo is better at
   champions.
4. These numbers form the honest reference point for the 2026 forecast:
   expect solid skill on qualification/quarter-final probabilities and
   wide error bars on champion odds.

## Caveats

- Two tournaments is a small sample and per-stage outcomes are correlated
  through the bracket; treat skills as indicative, not precise.
- Historical fixture files use nominal dates and synthetic match order
  (round-robin pairings with host-venue flags), which does not affect the
  simulation; group files carry no FIFA-ranking column, so the last-resort
  tie-break falls back to a deterministic proxy.

## Reproduce

```bash
worldcup-predictor backtest-tournament \
  --matches data/raw/international_results.csv \
  --groups data/worldcup/groups_2018.csv \
  --fixtures data/worldcup/fixtures_2018.csv \
  --actual data/worldcup/actual_2018.csv \
  --format wc32 --train-before 2018-06-14 --label wc2018 \
  --model-type dixon_coles \
  --team-output data/processed/tournament_backtest_2018_dixon_coles.csv
```

Swap the year (2022 starts 2022-11-20) and `--model-type` for the other
runs; per-team probability tables live in
`data/processed/tournament_backtest_<year>_<model>.csv`. The historical
groups/fixtures/actual files are generated by
`scripts/generate_historical_worldcup_files.py`.
